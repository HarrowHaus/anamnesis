# NAME ANALYSIS — deep pass over the research

*ANAMNESIS is the working title. This is a full mining pass across the glossary (61 terms), the symbol dictionary, and the lineage material for naming candidates. Renaming the build later is a find-replace — nothing depends on the name.*

**Scoring criteria:** on-thesis · memorable · sayable & spellable · ownable (domain/handle plausible) · un-cancellable · not crank.

---

## The front-runners

**EIKON** — Greek *εἰκών*, "image," the literal root of *icon*. The most exact possible name: the whole site is about images and learning to read them. Clean, modern, ownable. *(Note: a finance product uses "Eikon" — different domain, low collision risk for a culture site.)*

**HIEROPHANT** — *surfaced by the deep pass and arguably the best fit found.* Literally "one who displays sacred things" — the priest of the mysteries who **revealed the holy symbols to initiates**. That is precisely what this site does: it shows the uninitiated how to read the sacred symbols. Memorable, a real word, tarot/mystery resonance, no ethnic or crank baggage. Slightly longer; spelling is learnable.

**LUCIDA** — in astronomy, the **brightest star in a constellation**; also *lucidity*. Astro-theology + clarity + the waking, in one warm, beautiful, ownable word.

## The full mined field, by thread

### The image itself
- **EIKON** — image / icon (above).
- **EIDOLON** — Greek "image, phantom, apparition": the *engineered* image as projected phantom. Evocative, eerie.
- **EIDOS** — Greek "form, the seen" (Plato's Forms). On-thesis but abstract; close to *eidolon*.
- **SPECULUM** — Latin "mirror," from Fludd's plate *Integrae Naturae Speculum*. Ties to real source art and "as above, so below." *Caveat: strong medical-instrument connotation.*

### Seeing · light · waking (the promise to the reader)
- **LUCIDA** — brightest star + lucidity (above).
- **GNOSIS** — Greek "saving, revealed knowledge." The payoff the site offers: symbolic literacy as gnosis. Clean, known-ish.
- **ANAMNESIS** *(incumbent)* — Plato's "recollection," the soul waking to what it knew. Beautiful meaning; weak on spelling/recall.

### The method (reading beneath the surface)
- **PALIMPSEST** — a manuscript scraped and overwritten, the older text showing through. Exactly the method: reading the hidden layer beneath the visible one. Sophisticated; half-known.
- **MIMESIS** — Greek "imitation/representation"; Plato's worry about how images copy and distort the real (Pillar 1). On-thesis, academic in tone.
- **GRAMARYE** — archaic word for occult learning, and the literal root of **glamour** (originally an enchantment cast over the *eyes*). Astonishingly on-thesis — the engineered image *is* a glamour. Rich but obscure.

### The war on images (the edge)
- **THE GRAVEN** — from "graven images" and the Second Commandment; the spine of Pillar 2. One ominous, memorable word.
- **ICONOCLAST** — "image-breaker." Powerful, but means *destroying* images; the site reads them, so it's slightly off-thesis.

### The controller of the image (the antagonist seen straight)
- **DEMIURGE** — the craftsman who shapes the world-image; "whoever controls the image." Strong thesis fit; leans villain-heavy.
- **EGREGORE** — a collective thoughtform sustained by a group's shared attention. The engineered image *as* an egregore — directly on the "engineering of consciousness" thesis. Insider, distinctive, a little eerie.

### The revealer (the site's own stance)
- **HIEROPHANT** — the displayer of sacred things (above; top-tier).
- **LOGOS** — "word, reason, the ordering principle" — and the literal root of **logo**. Given the corporate-logo casebook, the double meaning is potent: the divine Word and the brand mark in one. *Caveat: common and overloaded; hard to own outright.*
- **SIGIL** — a charged symbol; a glyph condensing intention. Punchy, modern, exactly the object of study. *Caveat: heavily used in occult branding already.*

## Recommendation
Three genuinely strong, ownable directions by the feeling you want the front door to have:

- **EIKON** — leads with *the image*: clean, modern, exact.
- **HIEROPHANT** — leads with *the act of revealing*: names what the site does, with mystery-tradition weight. (The standout the deep pass turned up.)
- **LUCIDA** — leads with *waking / clarity*: warm and inviting.

If you want edge instead, **THE GRAVEN** (war on images) or **EGREGORE** (the engineered thoughtform) both brand hard and stay in-tradition.

My lean: **HIEROPHANT** or **EIKON**. Say which direction feels right and I'll pressure-test the top pick — domains/handles, how it reads as a logo and a one-liner, and whether it survives being said out loud.
