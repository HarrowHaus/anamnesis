Freeman — known publicly as Freeman Fly — built a media practice out of what others treated as private study. Working from radio and then television, he extended the Maxwell–Tsarion tradition into the landscape every viewer already inhabits: the corporate logo on the coffee cup, the halftime show, the award-ceremony stage. Freeman teaches that Hollywood is the holly-wood of Druidic wand-cutting, that entertainment is ritual, and that the symbols embedded in mass media shape collective consciousness below the threshold of critical awareness. His *Free Zone* broadcasts platformed nearly every voice in the tradition and made occult logo decoding a spectator practice available to anyone with a screen.
