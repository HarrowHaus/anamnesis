David Icke is the contemporary synthesizer who made Saturn the tradition's organizing symbol. Across his books and lectures he reads the cube, the hexagon, and the six-pointed star as a single Saturnian signature recurring through religion, Freemasonry, and corporate brand. In his "Saturn–Moon Matrix," Saturn is described as a broadcasting source amplified by the Moon, generating the "five-sense reality" we take for solid. Underneath the symbolism, Icke teaches that reality is consciousness — "infinite awareness having a human experience" — and that fear is the lever by which populations are kept controllable.
