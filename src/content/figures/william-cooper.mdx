William Cooper spent the 1990s transmitting, via shortwave, the case that ancient solar symbolism had never gone underground — that it ran the institutions openly, to those who knew how to read the signs. A U.S. Navy veteran turned broadcaster, Cooper produced the *Mystery Babylon* radio series: episode by episode, he walked listeners through the solar and zodiacal substrate of religion, the esoteric grammar of the great seal, and the initiatic logic of the degrees. His *Behold a Pale Horse* (1991) became one of the most-read books in the tradition. Cooper taught that the researcher's first obligation is rigorous personal verification — a methodological discipline he modeled, and passed on, in every broadcast.
