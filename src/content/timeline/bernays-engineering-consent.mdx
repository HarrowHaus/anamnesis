Edward Bernays — Sigmund Freud's nephew and a veteran of WWI propaganda — applied psychoanalytic and crowd-psychology ideas to peacetime persuasion. His books *Crystallizing Public Opinion* (1923) and *Propaganda* (1928) founded modern public relations. In *Propaganda* he wrote: "The conscious and intelligent manipulation of the organized habits and opinions of the masses is an important element in democratic society." His most famous stunt, the 1929 "Torches of Freedom" campaign branding women's cigarettes as feminist liberation, ran front-page in The New York Times — demonstrating that symbols could be manufactured and deployed to reshape public behavior at will.
