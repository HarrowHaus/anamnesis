Aniconism in Islamic religious art developed through the hadith collections of Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim, which warn image-makers they will be challenged to breathe life into their creations on the Day of Judgment. Sunni exegetes from the 9th century onward increasingly read these as categorical prohibitions on depicting living beings, fostering a magnificent tradition of calligraphy, geometric design, and arabesque that redirected an entire civilization's visual energy.
