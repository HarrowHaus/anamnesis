At its 25th session in December 1563, the Council of Trent issued its Decree on Sacred Images, reaffirming the legitimacy of icons against Protestant challenge while imposing rigorous new controls: bishops were charged with policing images for doctrinal correctness and decorum, banning lasciviousness and profane elements. Cardinal Gabriele Paleotti's 1582 manual codified the rules in detail. The result was not the suppression but the institutionalization of religious imagery — brought to heel and turned toward the state's emotional and doctrinal purposes.
