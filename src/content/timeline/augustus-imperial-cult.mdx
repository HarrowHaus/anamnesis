Augustus systematized state imagery at a scale never before attempted: statues placed him in temples of the imperial cult, coins declared him "son of a god," and a vast portrait program projected legitimacy to every corner of the empire. The Roman Imperial Coinage catalogue records some 550 distinct coin types issued between Actium (31 BCE) and Augustus's death (14 CE) — each one a portable, standardized image of divine authority.
