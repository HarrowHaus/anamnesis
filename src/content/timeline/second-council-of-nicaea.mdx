Empress Irene convened the Seventh Ecumenical Council at Nicaea, opening 24 September 787 with some 350 members and the presence of papal legates. It reversed the iconoclast Council of Hieria, decreeing that icons deserve honorific veneration (*proskynesis*) but not the true adoration (*latreia*) reserved for God alone, and that "the honor given to an icon passes through to its subject." The decree did not end conflict — a second wave of iconoclasm ran from 814 to 843 — but its theology of images endured.
