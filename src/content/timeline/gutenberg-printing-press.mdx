Around 1440 in Mainz, Johannes Gutenberg's movable-type press could produce up to 3,600 pages per day, compared with a few dozen by hand. By 1500, an estimated twenty million books had been printed across Europe. Images — reproduced primarily via woodcut — could now be replicated identically at scale, giving print-shop owners and their patrons new power to set the visual terms of religious and political debate.
