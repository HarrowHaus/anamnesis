Constantin-François Volney published *Les Ruines* in 1791, reading Christianity as an allegorical cult of the sun. Charles-François Dupuis followed in 1795 with the multi-volume *Origine de tous les cultes*, arguing through comparative analysis that gods and myths are allegories of the sun, moon, and constellations — presenting the life of Jesus as a "solar fable." Dupuis presented the work to fellow deputies on 7 September 1795; the Church placed it on the Index in 1827. Volney and Dupuis established the intellectual architecture that every subsequent astrotheology tradition built upon.
