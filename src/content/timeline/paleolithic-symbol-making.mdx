The oldest securely dated figurative cave paintings yet found are in the Maros-Pangkep karst of Sulawesi, Indonesia, where a 2024 study led by Maxime Aubert dated a narrative scene to at least 51,200 years old. In Europe, a red disk in El Castillo cave, Spain, is at least 40,800 years old. The deep, restricted locations of these images point from the very beginning to imagery tied to ritual and controlled access.
