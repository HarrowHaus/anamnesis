Reformed leaders Andreas Karlstadt, Huldrych Zwingli, and John Calvin invoked the Second Commandment's ban on idolatry to urge removal of religious images; iconoclastic riots swept Zürich (1523), Geneva (1535), and Scotland (1559). The largest wave, the Beeldenstorm of 10 August 1566, saw organized bands numbering around 3,000 ravage approximately 400 churches across the Low Countries in a single summer, the destruction inseparable from political revolt against Catholic Habsburg rule.
