As television entered most American homes in the 1950s, the persuasion techniques pioneered by Bernays and motivational researchers such as Ernest Dichter were applied to TV advertising and political broadcasting — including the 1952 Eisenhower "Eisenhower Answers America" spots. Mass broadcast created a shared daily ritual of commercially controlled imagery reaching tens of millions simultaneously, concentrating the power to set the terms of desire and political identity in a very few hands.
