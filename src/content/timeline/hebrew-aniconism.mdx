The Hebrew Bible's Second Commandment prohibits making "any graven image or any likeness" of anything in heaven, earth, or water. The ban gained practical force through the reforms of Kings Hezekiah and Josiah in the late 8th–7th centuries BCE, who destroyed cultic idols and centralized worship in Jerusalem — establishing image-prohibition as an instrument of political as well as religious consolidation.
