Around 726–730, Byzantine Emperor Leo III ordered the removal of an image of Christ from the Chalke Gate of the imperial palace in Constantinople, replacing it with a plain cross, and banned the veneration of religious icons as idolatry. His son Constantine V made the suppression official at the Council of Hieria (754), attended by 338 bishops, triggering widespread destruction of icons and persecution of those who venerated them.
