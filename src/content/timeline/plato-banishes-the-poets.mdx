In the Republic, Plato's Socrates argues across Books II–III and X that imitative poetry and art (*mimesis*) must be censored or banished from the ideal city. Imitation is "two steps removed" from reality, corrupts the soul by feeding its irrational parts, and even Homer — "the first teacher" — must be expelled. Plato has since been called the founding father of Western literary and image censorship.
