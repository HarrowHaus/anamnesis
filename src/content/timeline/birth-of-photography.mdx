Louis Daguerre's daguerreotype process was announced to the world in August 1839, with the French government presenting it as a "gift to humanity," while Henry Fox Talbot announced his competing negative-positive process the same year. Photography introduced the mechanically captured, apparently objective image — and almost immediately, photographic manipulation: staged scenes, retouched portraits, and propaganda photographs exploiting the camera's unique aura of documentary truth.
