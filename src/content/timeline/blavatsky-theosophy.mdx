Helena Petrovna Blavatsky co-founded the Theosophical Society in New York City on 17 November 1875 with Henry Steel Olcott and William Quan Judge. She published *Isis Unveiled* in 1877 — its first 1,000-copy print run sold out in two days — and her major work *The Secret Doctrine* in two volumes in 1888. Blavatsky presented Theosophy as "the synthesis of science, religion and philosophy," claiming to revive an Ancient Wisdom underlying all religions and interpreting the ouroboros, swastika, Star of David, and ankh as shared symbols of that tradition — the key transmission node between Enlightenment astrotheology and every subsequent wave of esoteric symbol-decoding.
