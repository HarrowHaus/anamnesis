Leni Riefenstahl's *Triumph of the Will*, commissioned to document the 1934 Nuremberg Nazi Party Congress, was released 28 March 1935; shot with 30 cameras and a crew of approximately 150, it transformed a rally of hundreds of thousands into a choreographed spectacle projecting the regime as a messianic order. The same year, Walter Benjamin drafted "The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction," whose epilogue concluded: "The logical outcome of Fascism is an aestheticizing of political life." Together, the film and the essay defined the modern problem of mechanically reproduced imagery in the service of state power.
