At age 27, Manly Palmer Hall self-published *The Secret Teachings of All Ages: An Encyclopedic Outline of Masonic, Hermetic, Qabbalistic and Rosicrucian Symbolical Philosophy*, illustrated by J. Augustus Knapp. The first Subscribers' edition was limited to 550 signed copies; its oversized format and comprehensive scope — covering Hermeticism, alchemy, Tarot, Pythagorean number, astrology, Freemasonry, and more — made it the foundational reference of the modern esoteric tradition. Hall argued that "concealed within the emblematic figures, allegories and rituals of the ancients, is a secret doctrine" and founded the Philosophical Research Society in Los Angeles in 1934 to continue the work.
