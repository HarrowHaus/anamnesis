Facebook launched in 2004; its News Feed launched 5 September 2006, shifting from static profiles to an aggregated, machine-ranked stream — provoking immediate backlash from 700,000+ users within days. The Like button (9 February 2009) introduced the core engagement metric; algorithmic ranking (EdgeRank) replaced chronological order with machine-curated content. Instagram adopted a non-chronological feed in 2016; Twitter followed. TikTok's fully recommender-driven "For You" page completed the shift, placing the decisions about which images and symbols reach which eyes entirely inside a private engagement-optimization system.
