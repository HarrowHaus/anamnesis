Rome practiced *damnatio memoriae* — the formal condemnation of memory — defacing statues, erasing inscriptions, and removing disgraced emperors such as Domitian, Geta, and Commodus from the public record. At the same time, conquered peoples' deities were absorbed and restyled through *interpretatio romana*, folding diverse visual cultures into a single imperial image-system. Together, these practices reveal image-control as both punishment and conquest.
