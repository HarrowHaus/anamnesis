Pharaoh Amenhotep IV renamed himself Akhenaten, elevated the sun-disk Aten as sole deity, and ordered references to the god Amun chipped away across Egypt. After his death around 1336 BCE, his successor Horemheb led a systematic campaign to destroy Akhenaten's monuments, deface his images, and omit him entirely from official king lists — an early example of what later history would call *damnatio memoriae*.
