A network of independent researchers transmitted the Dupuis–Blavatsky–Hall astrotheology and symbol-decoding tradition to late-20th and early-21st-century audiences through books, video, radio, and the early internet. Jordan Maxwell (born Russell Joseph Pine, 1940–2022) popularized "astrotheology" and the decoding of religious and corporate symbols as solar-cult survivals; Michael Tsarion and Freeman Fly advanced related symbol-decoding and hidden-ruler narratives. This material fed directly into the film *Zeitgeist* (2007), which drew on the astrotheological Christ-myth thesis — reaching millions of viewers and anchoring the tradition's claims firmly in the culture of the early internet age.
