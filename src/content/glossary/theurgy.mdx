definition: "From the Greek for 'divine working,' a set of ritual practices used by Neoplatonist philosophers — especially Iamblichus — to invoke the active presence of the gods and elevate the soul toward the divine. Theurgy holds that ritual action, not only contemplation, can effect genuine spiritual ascent and transformation."

Where Plotinus sought the One through pure intellect, Iamblichus insisted that matter itself must participate — that the gods descend through correct ritual as much as the soul ascends through philosophy.
