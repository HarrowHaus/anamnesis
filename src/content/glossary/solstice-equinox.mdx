definition: "The solstices are the two moments each year when the Sun reaches its farthest point north or south, producing the longest and shortest days (around June 21 and December 21). The equinoxes mark when day and night are equal (around March 21 and September 23). Both result from Earth's axial tilt and have been honored in religious and symbolic calendars worldwide since antiquity."
