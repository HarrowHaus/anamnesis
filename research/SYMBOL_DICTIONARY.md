## SOLAR / ASTRO

### Solar Cross (aka Sun Cross, Wheel Cross, Odin's Cross)
- Category: Solar/Astro
- Documented origin: An equal-armed cross within a circle, among the oldest recurring motifs in human art, appearing on Bronze Age European artifacts and prehistoric rock carvings; widely interpreted by scholars as representing the sun and the solar year divided into four parts (solstices and equinoxes).
- Esoteric reading: In the astrotheology tradition (Jordan Maxwell, Santos Bonacci), the wheel-cross is the original "cross" — the sun's annual path through the zodiac, with the cardinal points marking the equinoxes and solstices; the "crucifixion" is read as the sun "crossing" the celestial equator.
- Modern appearances: Celtic high crosses; the halo behind saints; automotive and corporate roundels; currency vignettes.
- Related symbols: Zodiac wheel, ankh, Christian cross, swastika.
- Decoded by: Jordan Maxwell, Santos Bonacci, D.M. Murdock, William Cooper.
- Sources: 1) Manly P. Hall, *The Secret Teachings of All Ages*, "The Sun, A Universal Deity" — https://sacred-texts.com/eso/sta/index.htm 2) Santos Bonacci, Universal Truth School — https://universaltruthschool.com/

### The Swastika (aka Sun Wheel, Fylfot, Gammadion, Hakenkreuz)
- Category: Solar/Astro
- Documented origin: An ancient cross-cultural geometric symbol used for thousands of years across Eurasia; the 19th-century Sanskrit scholar Monier Monier-Williams noted most scholars consider it originally a solar symbol [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swastika) (Sanskrit *svastika* = "well-being"). The earliest known depiction is carved on a small mammoth-ivory bird figurine discovered at Mezine, Ukraine, radiocarbon-dated to approximately 12,000–15,000 years ago (now in the National Museum of the History of Ukraine, Kyiv).
- Esoteric reading: In the astrotheology tradition, read as a depiction of the sun's rotation and the cosmos in motion — a "good fortune" solar glyph.
- Modern appearances: Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain temples, homes, and festival décor across Asia; ancient pottery and mosaics.
- Related symbols: Solar cross, sun wheel, Black Sun.
- Decoded by: Jordan Maxwell, Robert Sepehr, Manly P. Hall.
- Sources: 1) Wikipedia, "Swastika" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swastika 2) USHMM Holocaust Encyclopedia, "History of the Swastika" — https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/history-of-the-swastika

### The Zodiac Wheel
- Category: Solar/Astro
- Documented origin: The twelve-sign zodiac was developed in Babylonian astronomy in the first millennium BCE (each sign ~30° of the ecliptic) and transmitted through Greek astronomy into the Western tradition.
- Esoteric reading: Bonacci's syncretism holds that the zodiac is the master-key to all scripture — the 12 tribes of Israel and 12 disciples are the 12 signs, and "Is-Ra-El" encodes Isis-Ra-Saturn (El).
- Modern appearances: Cathedral floor mosaics, astronomical clocks, astrology media.
- Related symbols: Solar cross, fish/Pisces, the cross, lamb/Aries.
- Decoded by: Santos Bonacci, Jordan Maxwell, D.M. Murdock.
- Sources: 1) Santos Bonacci, "Astrotheology Summary" (Tull & Oldenkamp) — https://pdfcoffee.com/santos-bonacci-astrotheology-summary-pdf-free.html 2) Manly P. Hall, *The Secret Teachings of All Ages*, "The Zodiac and Its Signs" — https://sacred-texts.com/eso/sta/index.htm

### The Ankh (aka Crux Ansata, Key of Life, Key of the Nile)
- Category: Solar/Astro
- Documented origin: Egyptian hieroglyph for "life" (`nḫ), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ankh) attested from the First Dynasty; leading origin theories include Gardiner's sandal-strap and Wallis Budge's "knot of Isis" (a fertility girdle). [World History Encyclopedia](https://www.worldhistory.org/Ankh/) Coptic Christians later adopted it as the crux ansata. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ankh)
- Esoteric reading: In the astrotheology tradition (Jordan Maxwell, D.M. Murdock), read as a solar/fertility emblem uniting male and female principles (Osiris and Isis), the loop as the sun rising over the horizon-cross.
- Modern appearances: Coptic cross; jewelry; Kemetic neopaganism; goth subculture.
- Related symbols: Solar cross, winged sun disk, Eye of Horus.
- Decoded by: Jordan Maxwell, Manly P. Hall, D.M. Murdock.
- Sources: 1) World History Encyclopedia, "The Ankh" — https://www.worldhistory.org/Ankh/ 2) Wikipedia, "Ankh" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ankh

### The Winged Sun Disk (aka Behdety)
- Category: Solar/Astro
- Documented origin: Egyptian solar symbol — a sun disk flanked by outstretched falcon wings (often with two uraeus serpents) — attested from the Old Kingdom (Sneferu, 26th century BCE); associated with Horus of Behdet/Edfu, later identified with Ra-Horakhty. The motif spread across the Near East to Assyria, Anatolia, and Persia, giving rise to the Zoroastrian Faravahar.
- Esoteric reading: In the astrotheology tradition (Maxwell, Tsarion), the supreme emblem of the deified, flying sun — the visible god of the sky in motion.
- Modern appearances: Faravahar (Iran/Zoroastrianism); Masonic, theosophical, and Rosicrucian iconography.
- Related symbols: Ankh, Eye of Horus, solar cross, dove.
- Decoded by: Jordan Maxwell, Michael Tsarion, Manly P. Hall.
- Sources: 1) World History Encyclopedia, "Faravahar" — https://www.worldhistory.org/Faravahar/ 2) Wikipedia, "Winged sun" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winged_sun

### The Obelisk
- Category: Solar/Astro
- Documented origin: Egyptian monolithic tapering pillar sacred to the sun god Ra, associated with the benben stone of Heliopolis and the petrified rays of the sun; many were later transported to Rome and Western capitals.
- Esoteric reading: In the astrotheology/symbol-control tradition (Jordan Maxwell, William Cooper), read as a transplanted Egyptian solar-phallic emblem of the sun god marking centers of power.
- Modern appearances: Washington Monument; the obelisk in St. Peter's Square; Cleopatra's Needles (London, New York, Paris).
- Related symbols: Washington Monument, winged sun disk, Capitol dome.
- Decoded by: Jordan Maxwell, William Cooper, Michael Tsarion.
- Sources: 1) Manly P. Hall, *The Secret Teachings of All Ages* — https://sacred-texts.com/eso/sta/index.htm 2) Jordan Maxwell Official Archive — https://jordanmaxwell.org/

### Sol Invictus / The Halo-Nimbus
- Category: Solar/Astro
- Documented origin: *Sol Invictus* ("Unconquered Sun") was an official Roman sun cult (notably under Aurelian, 274 CE); the radiant nimbus/halo in art derives from classical solar-deity iconography (Sol/Helios/Apollo) and was adopted into Christian sacred art.
- Esoteric reading: D.M. Murdock and the astrotheology tradition read the halo as the literal disk of the sun rendered behind the head of the solar savior.
- Modern appearances: Christian sacred art; saints' and Christ's halos; civic sunburst motifs.
- Related symbols: Solar cross, winged sun disk, cross.
- Decoded by: D.M. Murdock, Jordan Maxwell, Santos Bonacci.
- Sources: 1) D.M. Murdock (Acharya S), *The Christ Conspiracy* — https://stellarhousepublishing.com/ 2) Manly P. Hall, "The Sun, A Universal Deity" — https://sacred-texts.com/eso/sta/index.htm

## SACRED GEOMETRY

### The Flower of Life
- Category: Sacred Geometry
- Documented origin: An overlapping-circles grid (nineteen circles in the classic form). The often-cited Abydos examples are red-ochre drawings on granite columns of the Temple of Osiris (Osireion); per analysis summarized by Wikipedia and researcher David Furlong, these can be no earlier than 535 BCE and most probably date to the 2nd–4th century CE, based on adjacent Greek text (including an inscription read as relating to "the god of the Nile") and the circles' position high on the columns — indicating they are later graffiti rather than part of the original Egyptian scheme.
- Esoteric reading: In the sacred-geometry tradition (popularized by modern teachers and cited by Santos Bonacci), the blueprint of creation containing the Seed of Life and, by extension, all five Platonic solids.
- Modern appearances: Jewelry, tattoos, meditation tools, New Age and decorative art.
- Related symbols: Vesica Piscis, Seed of Life, Metatron's Cube.
- Decoded by: Santos Bonacci, Manly P. Hall.
- Sources: 1) Wikipedia, "Overlapping circles grid" / Flower of Life — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overlapping_circles_grid 2) David Furlong, "The Osirion and the Flower of Life" — https://www.davidfurlong.co.uk/egypttour_osirion.html

### The Vesica Piscis
- Category: Sacred Geometry
- Documented origin: The pointed-oval ("lens") shape formed by two overlapping circles of equal radius whose centers each sit on the other's circumference; used in sacred architecture and as a geometric generator (its proportions yield √3) since antiquity.
- Esoteric reading: In the sacred-geometry tradition, the womb/portal of creation and the geometric root of the fish (Ichthys) symbol — the meeting of two worlds.
- Modern appearances: Gothic church windows and mandorlas; the Christian Ichthys; logos.
- Related symbols: Flower of Life, Ichthys, pentagram.
- Decoded by: Santos Bonacci, Manly P. Hall.
- Sources: 1) Manly P. Hall, *The Secret Teachings of All Ages* — https://sacred-texts.com/eso/sta/index.htm 2) Universal Truth School — https://universaltruthschool.com/

### The Golden Ratio / Fibonacci Spiral
- Category: Sacred Geometry

- Esoteric reading: In the sacred-geometry tradition, the "divine proportion" encoding the growth pattern of nature, the human body, and life itself ("as above, so below").
- Modern appearances: Architecture, graphic design, and logos claiming golden-ratio construction.
- Related symbols: Pentagram, Flower of Life, Sri Yantra.

## ALCHEMICAL

### The Ouroboros
- Category: Alchemical
- Documented origin: A serpent or dragon devouring its own tail; the earliest known depiction is in the Egyptian *Enigmatic Book of the Netherworld* in the tomb of Tutankhamun (14th century BCE); later a key Gnostic and alchemical emblem (notably in the Chrysopoeia of Cleopatra, "ἓν τὸ πᾶν" — "the all is one").
- Esoteric reading: In the alchemical tradition (Hall, Passio), the eternal cycle of death and renewal and the unity of all matter — the self-sustaining cosmos.
- Modern appearances: Logos, jewelry, infinity/eternity imagery.
- Related symbols: Serpent/kundalini, triple spiral, Flower of Life.
- Decoded by: Manly P. Hall, Mark Passio.
- Sources: 1) Manly P. Hall, *The Secret Teachings of All Ages* — https://sacred-texts.com/eso/sta/index.htm 2) Mark Passio, What On Earth Is Happening — https://www.whatonearthishappening.com/

Forwood; [ScienceInsights](https://scienceinsights.org/what-is-the-rod-of-asclepius-meaning-and-origin/) the AMA later switched to the Rod of Asclepius (1912). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caduceus_as_a_symbol_of_medicine)

- Modern appearances: Commercial and (erroneously) medical logos; the U.S. Surgeon General's flag.
- Related symbols: Rod of Asclepius, serpent/kundalini, Baphomet.
- Decoded by: Jordan Maxwell, Manly P. Hall, Mark Passio.
- Sources: 1) Wikipedia, "Caduceus as a symbol of medicine" — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caduceus_as_a_symbol_of_medicine 2) NIH PMC, "Caduceus and Asclepius: A Tale of two Rods" — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9582002/

- Modern appearances: Alchemical and occult literature; tattoos; emblem design.
- Related symbols: Hexagram, Rebis, philosopher's stone.

## FRATERNAL / MASONIC

### The Square and Compasses
- Category: Fraternal/Masonic
- Documented origin: The principal emblem of Freemasonry, combining the stonemasons' square and compasses (often enclosing the letter G), standardized as the Craft's identifying symbol through the 18th–19th centuries.
- Esoteric reading: Hall reads the square (matter/earth/body) and the compasses (spirit/heaven/soul) as the union of opposites and the "squaring" of human conduct toward virtue.
- Modern appearances: Masonic lodges, rings, aprons, and building cornerstones worldwide.
- Related symbols: Letter G, blazing star, all-seeing eye.
- Decoded by: Manly P. Hall, Jordan Maxwell, Mark Passio.
- Sources: 1) Manly P. Hall, "Freemasonic Symbolism," *The Secret Teachings of All Ages* — https://sacred-texts.com/eso/sta/index.htm 2) Jordan Maxwell Official Archive — https://jordanmaxwell.org/
