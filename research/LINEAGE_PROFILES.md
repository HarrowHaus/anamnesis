## ANCESTORS / SOURCES

### Gerald Massey
- **Dates & status:** May 29, 1828 – October 29, 1907 (England). Deceased.
- **Tier:** Ancestor
- **One-line:** English poet and self-taught Egyptologist who argued Christianity derived from Egyptian myth.
- **Role in lineage:** Original source for the "Egypt → Christianity" and Horus–Jesus thesis cited throughout the modern scene.
- **Core claims:**
  - Massey taught that the Gospel narrative was largely inspired by Egyptian mythology, with Jesus essentially based on the Egyptian sky-god Horus.
  - Massey taught that biblical place-names and events are "astronomical mythology" (celestial, not geographical) — e.g., the 40 days/years derived from Egyptian seed/Osiris symbolism.
  - In Massey's view, the early religions had their myths *interpreted*, while moderns inherited theirs *misinterpreted* as literal history.
  - Massey emphasized the zodiac, the precession of the equinoxes, and solar/lunar cycles as the substrate of scripture.
- **Key works:** *A Book of the Beginnings* (1881); *The Natural Genesis* (1883); *Ancient Egypt: The Light of the World* (1907); *Gerald Massey's Lectures*. https://books.google.com/books/about/The_Historical_Jesus_and_the_Mythical_Ch.html?id=vv2XUnaM91EC
- **Signature examples:** Horus–Jesus parallels; "Equinoctial Christolatry"; Egyptian origin of the cross, virgin birth and resurrection imagery.
- **Influenced by:** Godfrey Higgins; Samuel Birch and the British Museum Egyptologists | **Influenced:** Alvin Boyd Kuhn, Jordan Maxwell, Michael Tsarion, D.M. Murdock, *Zeitgeist*.
- **Sources:** 1. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gerald_Massey 2. https://www.thechristmyth.com/massey.html 3. https://stellarhousepublishing.com/who-is-gerald-massey/

### Carl Jung
- **Dates & status:** July 26, 1875 – June 6, 1961 (Switzerland). Deceased.

- **One-line:** Swiss psychiatrist; founder of analytical psychology; theorist of archetypes and the collective unconscious.
- **Core claims (his actual work):**

  - Jung studied alchemy, mythology and Gnosticism as symbolic maps of psychological transformation ("individuation").

- **Influenced by:** Sigmund Freud (then diverged), Gnosticism, alchemy | **Influenced:** Joseph Campbell; Mark Passio and the symbol-decode scene (interpretively).
